Welcome!

I am an experimental mineral physicist interested in understanding the chemical and mineralogical composition of the Earth′s deep interior, and how materials from the Earth′s surface are recycled into the deep mantle. I conduct high-pressure and high-temperature experiments in the multi-anvil press and in the diamond anvil cell to investigate the physical properties, crystal chemistry, and phase equilibria of nominally anhydrous (e.g., olivine, ringwoodite, stishovite, bridgmanite, CaFe2O4-type aluminous phase) and hydrous (dense hydrous silicates and aluminum oxyhydroxides) mineral phases. A combination of diffraction (single-crystal and powdered samples, in house and at synchrotrons) and spectroscopic techniques (Raman, infrared, Mössbauer spectroscopy, Brillouin scattering) both at ambient conditions and in situ at high pressures and temperatures, allows me to determine mineral properties and model the behavior of candidate mantle rocks over pressure and temperature ranges relevant to planetary interiors.
